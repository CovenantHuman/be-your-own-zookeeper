So Everything Still Feels Bad?

Here are a few more things to think about/try:

Try taking a few deep breathes.

Being uncomfortable often makes us hold our breathe. Try breathing conciously for a few minutes.

If it's daytime and you haven't gotten dressed, try putting on some clothes.

Sometimes dressing up as though you feel better, can help make it so.

Relatedly, even if you are already dressed, you can consider changing into clothes that make you happier or doing something else that makes you happier with your appearance.

Sometimes it's a matter of faking it until you make it and appearance can be a big part of that.

If you work with a professional for this kind of feeling, now might be a good time to reach out to them.
If not there's always the option of reaching out to a crisis line.

The National Alliance on Mental Illness recommends:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline - 800-273-8255
  • Crisis Text Line - Text NAMI to 741-741
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline - 800-799-7233
  • National Sexual Assault Hotline - 800-656-4673

These are just a few of the resources that are available. A quick internet search will supply more, if you need them.

And if you figure out what's wrong about right now, remember that you can always add another question to the checklist for next time.
